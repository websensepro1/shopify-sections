Whatever the reason, you went to cancel your Shopify subscription and… hit a wall. That “Cancel Plan” button seems to have vanished.

## Step 1:

Where to Find the Actual Cancel Button
First, you need the right permissions. Only the store owner (the admin) can cancel the plan. If you’re a staff member, you won’t even see the option.

If you’re the admin, here’s the path:

Log into your Shopify admin.
Click on the Settings gear icon (usually in the bottom-left corner).
Click on Plan.
There it is! You’ll see the “Cancel plan” button.

## Step 2:

Your Two Options: Pause or Cancel
Shopify gives you a helpful choice here:

Pause your store: This is a fantastic option if you think you’ll be back. For $9/month, your checkout is disabled, but you can still access your admin to edit products, view analytics, and keep your store’s design. All your data stays safe.
Cancel completely: This closes your store permanently at the end of your billing cycle. You’ll lose access after that date.

## Step 3:

The Crucial Steps Before You Click “Continue”
This is the most important part to avoid surprise charges. Your Shopify subscription is separate from other services.

Cancel Your Apps: Shopify isn’t responsible for the apps you’ve installed. Go to Apps > Installed apps and manually uninstall any paid third-party apps. Otherwise, those developers may continue to charge you.
Handle Your Domain: If you bought your domain through Shopify, canceling your store does not cancel your domain registration. You need to go to Settings > Domains and manage that subscription separately to avoid losing your domain name.
Once you’ve done those two things, you can safely proceed with cancellation. Shopify will show you the exact date your access will end, giving you time to download any reports, product info, or images.
